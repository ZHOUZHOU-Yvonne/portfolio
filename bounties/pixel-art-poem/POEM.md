# The Compiler's Lament

## I. The Build

In silent halls where circuits hum their song,
The engineer sits patient through the night,
Compiling dreams in code both swift and strong,
Awaiting signals on the screen so bright.

The cursor blinks, a metronome of thought,
Each keystroke paints a logic yet unformed,
A universe from abstractions wrought,
Where functions dance and data streams are warmed.

---

## II. The Error

But lo! The terminal erupts in red—
A traceback scrolls like thunder through the mind,
A semicolon missing, left for dead,
The bug no documentation helped to find.

The stack unwinds its tale of woe and grief,
Through layers deep where no light penetrates,
The engineer, beyond all disbelief,
Finds peace in problems that the craft creates.

---

## III. The Fix

A single line, a change so small, so slight,
The typo quelled, the logic now aligned,
The build runs green, the darkness turns to light,
The simplest answers are the last to find.

So raise a glass to those who shape the bytes,
Whose patience outlasts every segfault cry—
The silent poets of the sleepless nights,
Who teach the silicon to reach the sky.

---

*Creative decision: I chose a three-stanza narrative arc mirroring the developer's journey — build, break, fix — using classical English sonnet form (ABAB CDCD EFEF) to contrast the structured nature of code with the human emotional experience of creating it. The theme honors the unsung craftsmanship of software engineering.*
